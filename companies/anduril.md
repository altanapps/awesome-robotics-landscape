# Anduril Industries
**Vertical:** Defense / dual-use robotics | **URL:** https://www.anduril.com | **HQ:** Costa Mesa, CA | **Founded:** 2017 | **Stage/Funding:** Late-stage private; $6.29B raised across 7 rounds; $30.5B valuation (Jun 2025), reportedly targeting $60B (Mar 2026)

## Product
Anduril sells autonomous defense hardware and the Lattice OS software platform that connects them. Hardware SKUs include: Altius (tube-launched fixed-wing UAS), Anvil (counter-UAS interceptor quadcopter), Ghost (surveillance VTOL), and Roadrunner (reusable interceptor). Lattice is an AI-powered C2/command layer that fuses sensor feeds, manages autonomous assets, and supports human-machine teaming. The company is also building Arsenal-1, a hyperscale manufacturing campus near Columbus, OH, and opened a solid rocket motor factory in Mississippi in August 2025, making it one of only three US SRM suppliers.

## ICP
Primary buyer is the US Department of Defense — SOCOM, Army, Air Force — and allied governments including the Australian Defence Force and UK Ministry of Defence. Deal sizes range from $86M (SOCOM autonomy integration) to a $20B decade-long IDIQ for Lattice C2 across federal agencies. Secondary buyer is DHS for border surveillance. All sales are government-to-government or direct enterprise; no commercial or SMB segment.

## Traction
Sacra estimates $1B revenue in 2024, $2.1B in 2025 (110% YoY), and projects $4.3B in 2026. Key contracts: $86M SOCOM Mission Autonomy Systems Integration Partner (2025); $87M Army JIATF-401 Lattice C2 contract (2026) anchoring a potential $20B IDIQ; Army IBCS-M fire-control platform award (Nov 2025). Topped CNBC Disruptor 50 in 2025. Sources: Sacra (2025), DefenseScoop (2025–2026).

## Tech moat
Lattice OS integration depth across sensor-to-shooter workflows creates switching costs once embedded in a command stack. Arsenal-1 is positioning Anduril as a vertically integrated manufacturer, rare for a defense tech startup. SRM production capability is a genuine bottleneck asset. However, core autonomy algorithms are not published as proprietary IP and face replication risk from Shield AI and Rebellion Defense. Regulatory ITAR/FMS moat is real but shared by all US prime contractors.

## Distribution model
Direct enterprise sales to government procurement offices (DoD, DHS, allied MoDs). Sales motion is relationship-driven and program-of-record-oriented, requiring multi-year BD cycles. The Lattice Partner Program enables third-party hardware to integrate onto the platform, creating a soft ecosystem/marketplace layer. No RaaS or channel model.

## Critical weakness
Anduril is burning >$800M/year ahead of Arsenal-1 revenue recognition, making it dependent on large contract milestones. An October 2025 Army memo flagged security concerns with autonomous systems in contested environments, signaling reliability risk at scale. The company has no meaningful commercial revenue stream — a single DoD budget cycle freeze or continuing resolution could stall growth. International expansion is gated by FMS/ITAR approval timelines.

## Adjacent gap
Anduril's ICP is exclusively Tier-1 nation-state militaries with multi-year procurement cycles — it cannot and does not serve the market of NATO/EU mid-tier allies (Poland, Romania, Baltic states, Nordic countries) that urgently need affordable, rapidly deployable autonomous surveillance and counter-UAS capability but lack the procurement infrastructure to navigate US FMS. A startup building a Lattice-lite C2 layer and attritable drone stack purpose-built for EU/NATO mid-tier procurement frameworks (OCCAR, EDA, direct commercial sale) — with compliance pre-baked and price points an order of magnitude lower — could own that wedge before Anduril's sales cycle reaches it.
