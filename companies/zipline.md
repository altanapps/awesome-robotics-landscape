# Zipline
**Vertical:** Last-mile / sidewalk / road delivery (drones) | **URL:** https://www.flyzipline.com | **HQ:** South San Francisco, CA | **Founded:** 2014 | **Stage/Funding:** Series H — $1.63B total raised; $7.6B valuation (Jan 2026)

## Product
Zipline operates two hardware platforms. Platform 1 (P1) is a fixed-wing drone used for long-range logistics (up to 120-mile round trips), primarily serving medical supply chains in Africa and healthcare systems in the US. Platform 2 (P2) is a hover-capable system with a tethered "droid" that lowers payloads to a precise landing point — targeting suburban home delivery within a 10-mile radius with consistent ~10-minute delivery times and up to 8 lb payloads. Customers access delivery via a consumer app or through retail/restaurant integrations. The company operates its own hardware, software, and logistics stack end-to-end.

## ICP
Two segments: (1) Healthcare systems (Mayo Clinic, WellSpan Health, Memorial Hermann) paying for same-day/urgent medical supply and prescription delivery in the US and Africa — deal sizes likely enterprise contracts in the low-to-mid seven figures annually. (2) Retail/QSR chains (Walmart, Panera, Chipotle, Wendy's, Crumbl) using Zipline as a delivery channel for last-mile consumer orders in US suburban markets. Geography: US (TX, AR, WA, AZ, OH), Rwanda, Ghana, Kenya, Nigeria, Ivory Coast, Japan.

## Traction
Surpassed 2 million cumulative commercial deliveries as of January 2026; US home delivery volume grew ~15% week-over-week for seven consecutive months leading into Jan 2026; 2M deliveries milestone reached roughly 12 months after crossing 1M (Jan 2025). 120M+ autonomous miles flown total. (Sources: TechCrunch Jan 2026, The Robot Report Jan 2026.)

## Tech moat
Eleven years of autonomous flight data across 120M+ miles is the primary moat — training data for edge-case handling that new entrants cannot shortcut. P2's precision droid lowering system eliminates the need for a cleared landing pad, which is a meaningful UX differentiator versus competitors. FAA BVLOS waivers obtained (2023) represent regulatory lead time. Hardware is proprietary and manufactured in-house. No meaningful network-effect moat; distribution is merchant-by-merchant.

## Distribution model
Dual motion: (1) Enterprise direct sales to healthcare systems and large retail chains (Walmart-anchor); (2) Consumer-facing app for home delivery in served zip codes. The retail partnerships function as a channel — Zipline fulfills orders placed on the merchant's own platform. No OEM or marketplace model. Sales cycle is long; expansion is hub-by-hub, requiring FAA coordination per geography.

## Critical weakness
Unit economics remain challenged — estimated ~$13.50 per drone delivery versus ~$2 for ground vehicle delivery (Carrier Management, 2025). At current payload limits and range, Zipline cannot serve dense urban environments (buildings, no clearance) or high-weight categories. Expansion pace is throttled by FAA airspace approval, which is jurisdiction-specific and slow. A single regulatory setback in a key market (or a high-profile safety incident) can halt momentum. Deep retail partnerships depend on a small number of anchor accounts — losing Walmart or a top QSR chain would materially damage the US thesis.

## Adjacent gap
Zipline is structurally locked out of dense urban cores (apartment buildings, no yards, no safe descent clearance) and focuses entirely on B2C home delivery or large enterprise healthcare. The unserved wedge is **urban high-rise last-mile** — specifically, delivery to rooftop or balcony-accessible multi-family buildings in dense metros (NYC, Chicago, London). A competitor using a smaller, quieter airframe purpose-built for rooftop or window-adjacent delivery — with a different FAA/CAA regulatory path (lower altitude, shorter range, lighter payload) — could attack the urban apartment dweller segment that Zipline's P2 droid architecture physically cannot reach. This segment has high order frequency, strong willingness to pay for speed, and no credible drone incumbent.
