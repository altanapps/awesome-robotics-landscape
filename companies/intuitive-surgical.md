# Intuitive Surgical
**Vertical:** Surgical / medical robotics | **URL:** https://www.intuitive.com | **HQ:** Sunnyvale, CA, USA | **Founded:** 1995 | **Stage/Funding:** Public (NASDAQ: ISRG); market cap ~$170B (Apr 2026)

## Product
Intuitive sells the da Vinci surgical system — a multi-arm robotic platform for minimally invasive soft-tissue surgery — plus per-procedure instruments/accessories and a service contract layer. Da Vinci 5, launched in 2024, is the current flagship. Revenue follows a razor-and-blades structure: the capital system (robot placement) opens the account; instruments and accessories consumed per procedure, plus service contracts, generate ~85% of total revenue on a recurring basis. Intuitive also sells the Ion endoluminal system for peripheral lung biopsy.

## ICP
Large acute-care hospitals and health systems, primarily in the US (majority of revenue) and secondarily in Europe and Japan. Deal size: capital systems range from roughly $1M–$2.5M per unit; annual instrument and service spend per installed system typically adds several hundred thousand dollars. Reimbursement-dependent buyers — procurement cycles are 12–24 months and involve C-suite, surgical department heads, and value analysis committees.

## Traction
2025 revenue: $10.06B (+21% YoY). Installed base: 11,106 da Vinci systems globally as of Dec 31, 2025 (+12% YoY). 2025 procedure volume: ~3.15M (+18% YoY). 1,721 systems placed in 2025, of which 870 were da Vinci 5. (Source: Intuitive Q4 2025 earnings release, Jan 2026.)

## Tech moat
Four compounding layers: (1) **Regulatory/FDA clearance depth** — 25+ years of 510(k) and PMA filings across dozens of procedure types creates a large re-clearance burden for entrants; (2) **Surgeon training lock-in** — credentialing and proctoring programs mean surgeons trained on da Vinci resist switching; (3) **Proprietary instrument ecosystem** — single-use instruments are da Vinci-specific, generating captive recurring revenue; (4) **Outcomes data and AI tooling** — the My Intuitive platform and Intraoperative Insight analytics accumulate procedure data at scale no new entrant can replicate quickly. Manufacturing scale and supply-chain depth also present a meaningful cost barrier.

## Distribution model
Primarily direct sales force selling capital systems to hospital procurement; direct clinical specialists support surgeon onboarding and ongoing utilization. In Europe, Intuitive is acquiring its own distributors (Italy, Spain, Portugal deals announced for 2026 completion), moving toward full direct coverage. China operates via a joint venture (Intuitive-Fosun). No meaningful channel or marketplace motion — relationship-driven enterprise sales with multiyear service contracts anchoring retention.

## Critical weakness
Price and capital intensity exclude the fastest-growing care-delivery segment: ambulatory surgery centers (ASCs). A da Vinci system costs $1M–$2.5M upfront, requires dedicated OR infrastructure, and generates ROI only at high procedure volumes that most ASCs cannot sustain. As surgical volume shifts outpatient — accelerated by payer pressure and same-day procedure mandates — Intuitive has no competitive SKU for that environment. Competitors (CMR Surgical's Versius, emerging ASC-focused entrants) are explicitly targeting this gap with modular, lower-footprint, lower-cost systems.

## Adjacent gap
The ASC and lower-acuity hospital market in the US and Western Europe is structurally underserved. Intuitive's system economics require high-volume, well-capitalized hospital buyers; the 6,000+ US ASCs performing general surgery, gynecology, and urology procedures are priced out or capacity-constrained. A startup offering a purpose-built robotic system with a Robot-as-a-Service (RaaS) or per-procedure pricing model — eliminating the capital commitment — could wedge into this segment without competing head-on with da Vinci's installed base at major hospitals. The wedge is tighter still for single-specialty ASC chains (e.g., urology or colorectal) where procedure standardization reduces the training burden and a focused instrument set lowers regulatory scope.
