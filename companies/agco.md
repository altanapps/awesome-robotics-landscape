# AGCO / PTx Trimble
**Vertical:** Agricultural robotics | **URL:** https://www.agcocorp.com | **HQ:** Duluth, Georgia, USA | **Founded:** AGCO 1990; PTx Trimble JV closed April 2024 | **Stage/Funding:** Public (NYSE: AGCO); PTx Trimble JV valued at ~$2B (AGCO holds 85%, Trimble 15%), financed via $1.1B senior notes + $500M term loan

## Product
PTx Trimble is AGCO's precision-ag and autonomy vehicle — a brand-agnostic retrofit platform covering guidance, steering, and autonomous operation across mixed OEM fleets (supports ~40 brands, 10,000+ equipment models). The headline autonomy product is **OutRun**, the first commercially available retrofit autonomous grain-cart solution: it converts existing row-crop tractors (currently John Deere 8R/8000R IVT; Fendt planned 2026) into autonomous machines for harvest unloading, directly addressing seasonal labor shortages. The broader PTx stack includes GNSS receivers with Trimble's ProPoint engine, the AG-392 display, and digital farming software (FarmENGAGE, NextFarming, Panorama). AGCO targets consolidated precision-ag revenue exceeding $2B by 2028–29.

## ICP
Large-to-mid commercial row-crop operators in North America and Europe running mixed-brand fleets, with a secondary focus on former CNHi/OEM-channel customers who bought Trimble guidance hardware through Case IH or New Holland dealers. Deal motion is B2B through independent precision-ag dealers and AGCO's own dealer network. No published per-unit deal-size, but retrofit kits are premium hardware plays; Q1 2025 PTx Trimble revenue was ~$60M (annualized ~$240M run-rate).

## Traction
PTx Trimble reported $60M revenue in Q1 2025. OutRun won the Davidson Prize at Commodity Classic 2025 and Fast Company's World Changing Ideas Award (June 2025). AGCO is actively ramping its existing dealer network to carry PTx contracts after a Q4 2024 leadership restructure that accelerated synergies into Q1 2025. OutRun is in limited commercial release heading toward full availability in 2026. (Sources: AGCO Q1 2025 earnings; AGCO press releases 2025.)

## Tech moat
Trimble's 20+ years of precision-ag GNSS IP, the ProPoint multi-constellation positioning engine (GPS/GLONASS/Galileo/BeiDou), and a compatibility matrix spanning 10,000+ equipment models constitute the core defensible asset. Coupling this with AGCO's Fendt manufacturing and global dealer footprint creates a distribution moat difficult for pure-software entrants to replicate. The retrofit approach — decoupling autonomy from new-tractor purchases — also builds a data flywheel across brands without requiring fleet lock-in.

## Distribution model
Transitioning from OEM-embedded channel (CNHi co-branded Trimble hardware) to a dedicated independent precision-ag dealer network plus AGCO's own branded dealerships. Sales motion is dealer-led with hardware sale + software subscription overlay. Global footprint: North America primary, with European localization (German, French, Italian, Spanish, Portuguese portals).

## Critical weakness
The dealer-channel transition is the acute vulnerability: former CNHi customers must find new independent dealers for service and upgrades, creating a window of customer confusion and churn. Large-OEM rivals (Deere, CNH) can use this displacement period to lock farmers into proprietary systems. Additionally, OutRun's initial Deere-tractor dependency (8R/8000R only at launch) limits addressable market until Fendt and other brand support ships — meaning the autonomy revenue story depends heavily on a 2026+ hardware rollout that has not yet been validated at scale.

## Adjacent gap
PTx Trimble is built for large North American and European row-crop operations with modern, high-horsepower fleets and dealer proximity. The credible wedge is **smallholder and mid-scale arable farms in emerging markets** (Latin America, Southeast Asia, Sub-Saharan Africa) and **specialty-crop producers** (vegetables, orchards, vineyards) globally — segments requiring lower-cost, lighter-footprint autonomy that PTx's enterprise GNSS hardware and dealer-dependent service model cannot profitably reach. A startup could attack with lower-cost vision-first autonomy (camera + edge compute, no RTK dependency), direct-to-farmer SaaS distribution, and a service model that does not require a physical dealer within driving distance. The retrofit framing is proven; the gap is doing it affordably at smaller scale.
