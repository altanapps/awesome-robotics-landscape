# Vicarious Surgical
**Vertical:** Surgical / medical robotics | **URL:** https://www.vicarioussurgical.com | **HQ:** Waltham, MA, USA | **Founded:** 2014 | **Stage/Funding:** Public (NYSE: RBOT, delisted 2025/26); ~$220M raised via SPAC + subsequent equity offerings; $11.7M post-IPO round Oct 2025; going concern warning as of mid-2025

## Product
Vicarious Surgical is developing a single-port robotic surgical system for minimally invasive soft-tissue procedures, with ventral hernia repair as the lead indication. The system comprises a patient cart and surgeon console; instruments enter through a 1.5 cm incision, with 28 sensors per robotic arm and 360° 3D visualization. It is not FDA-cleared and not commercially available; the company targets an FDA submission in mid-2026. It holds FDA Breakthrough Device Designation.

## ICP
Target customers are U.S. acute-care hospitals and ambulatory surgical centers performing general surgery, specifically hernia repair. No international commercial plans disclosed. Deal structure is not public; given the market, capital-sale or RaaS models are typical. No paying customers exist yet.

## Traction
Zero revenue as of full-year 2025. Operating loss of ~$29M (H1 2025). Cash position ~$24M as of June 2025, with projected 2026 burn of ~$19M. No clinical patients treated as of early 2026; first-in-human trials remain pending FDA submission. (Sources: MacroTrends, MedTech Dive, MassDevice, 2025.)

## Tech moat
FDA Breakthrough Device Designation provides a faster regulatory pathway. The single-port, sub-2 cm incision architecture is differentiated from multi-port incumbents (Intuitive Surgical) and from other single-port entrants. 28-sensor arm replication is a proprietary hardware design. However, no cleared product exists, no clinical data has been published, and no manufacturing scale has been demonstrated — the moat is entirely prospective.

## Distribution model
Planned direct capital sale or RaaS to U.S. hospitals; sales motion not yet operational. Becton Dickinson and unnamed hospital chains in the U.S. and Asia are listed as investors, suggesting a channel/partnership distribution intent, but no formal distribution agreement is public.

## Critical weakness
The company faces near-term existential cash risk: ~$24M cash against ~$19M projected 2026 burn, with FDA submission not yet filed and first commercial revenue years away. NYSE delisted the stock under market-cap requirements. Multiple timeline delays (FDA submission pushed repeatedly from 2023 through 2026) erode investor confidence and make future capital raises costly or dilutive. A single further delay could exhaust runway before clearance.

## Adjacent gap
Vicarious is locked into the U.S. general surgery/hernia market and has no near-term path to international markets or adjacent soft-tissue indications (colorectal, gynecology). The deeper wedge: **outpatient and rural ASC markets in markets with lower regulatory barriers (EU MDR pathway, UKCA)**. A startup with a simpler, already-cleared single-port instrument or a tele-operated robotic assist (not a full surgical robot) could capture high-volume, low-acuity procedures — hernia, appendectomy — in under-robotized community hospitals and ASCs that can't afford Intuitive's $2M+ capital cost, without waiting for a full FDA 510(k) cycle.
