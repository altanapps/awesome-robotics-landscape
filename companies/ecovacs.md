# Ecovacs
**Vertical:** Domestic / consumer robotics | **URL:** https://www.ecovacs.com | **HQ:** Suzhou, China | **Founded:** 1998 | **Stage/Funding:** Public (SSE: 603486); $20M raised pre-IPO; market cap ~$5.24B (April 2026)

## Product
Ecovacs sells a range of consumer cleaning robots under the DEEBOT (robotic vacuum/mop combos), WINBOT (window cleaners), and GOAT (robotic lawn mowers) sub-brands. Flagship SKUs such as the DEEBOT X12 and T90 PRO OMNI feature OZMO Roller mopping technology, SLAM-based navigation, AI obstacle avoidance, and self-emptying/self-cleaning base stations. The product line spans entry-level (~$200) to premium (~$1,500+). All devices are cloud-connected and managed via the ECOVACS Home app.

## ICP
Mass-market homeowners in China and increasingly Western markets (US, Germany, UK, Australia). Core buyer: dual-income households willing to pay $300–$1,200 for automated floor care. China accounts for ~55% of revenue; overseas 45%. No enterprise or commercial cleaning segment.

## Traction
FY2025 revenue: ~19 billion CNY (~$2.6B USD), up 15% YoY. Net profit: ~1.76 billion CNY (~$257M), up 118% YoY. Q1 2026 revenue: ~4.9 billion CNY (~$716M), up 27% YoY. Products available in ~180 markets; 38 million+ households served globally. #1 robotic vacuum market share in China for 10 consecutive years. Sources: FastBull (2025), MarketScreener (2026).

## Tech moat
2,500+ patents covering SLAM navigation, AI algorithms, motor and sensor design, and IoT integration. Vertically integrated manufacturing in China provides cost leverage. Ten-year dominance in China creates proprietary training data for navigation/obstacle AI. Matter ecosystem certification and UL IoT security verification add minor distribution lock-in. Moat is real but not deep: core robotics IP (SLAM, suction, mopping) is broadly replicated by Roborock and Dreame.

## Distribution model
Balanced online/offline: direct-to-consumer via ecovacs.com and major e-commerce platforms (Amazon, Tmall, JD.com), plus retail shelf presence in big-box stores. Sales subsidiaries in US, Germany, Japan, and Singapore handle regional go-to-market. No RaaS or subscription revenue of note. Motion is largely transactional/product-led with influencer and review-site seeding.

## Critical weakness
Documented security vulnerabilities (DEF CON 2024): researchers demonstrated remote Bluetooth takeover of camera and microphone from up to 450 feet; Ecovacs initially refused to patch after disclosure. Cloud-dependent architecture with no local-processing option compounds the trust problem. In Western markets increasingly sensitive to Chinese data handling (post-iRobot/Scale AI controversy), this is an existential reputational risk that Roborock is already exploiting by emphasizing on-device image deletion.

## Adjacent gap
Ecovacs has no offering for the privacy-first, local-AI segment: buyers who want full home automation integration and guaranteed zero cloud data egress — a real and growing cohort of tech-savvy homeowners, Home Assistant users, and privacy-conscious Europeans under GDPR scrutiny. A startup offering a premium robot vacuum with fully on-device inference, open local API, and no mandatory cloud account could own this wedge; the install base of Home Assistant alone exceeds 1 million active users globally, and no incumbent cleanly serves it. Secondary gap: commercial/light-industrial cleaning (offices, small retail) remains unaddressed by all three Chinese incumbents who are locked into the consumer identity.
