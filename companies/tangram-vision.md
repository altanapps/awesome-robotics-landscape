# Tangram Vision
**Vertical:** Picks-and-shovels | **URL:** https://www.tangramvision.com | **HQ:** Boulder, CO (originally; also listed Broomfield, CO) | **Founded:** 2020 | **Stage/Funding:** Seed — $1.52M (Mar 2021; investors: 2048 Ventures lead, Trucks VC, Dynamo Ventures, SHAKTI, NASA SBIR grant $143K in 2024)

## Product
Tangram Vision sells MetriCal, a multi-modal sensor calibration suite for cameras, LiDAR, and IMUs. The core SKU is a credit-based licensed software tool that automates calibration target detection, intrinsic/extrinsic computation, distortion modeling, and temporal alignment across sensor arrays. An Enterprise tier provides white-label packaging, unlimited calibrations, and dedicated SLA support. The company also offers perception consulting as a services line. MetriCal integrates with ROS and OpenCV pipelines and targets production-line throughput (claiming >20x calibration speedup vs. manual processes and sub-mm accuracy).

## ICP
Perception and robotics engineers at hardware-producing companies building systems that require multi-sensor fusion — autonomous vehicles, defense robotics, industrial automation, and drone platforms. Named customers include Saronic Technologies, Wayve AI, and Kodiak. Geography appears primarily US-centric based on investor base and customer logos. Deal size is not published; Enterprise tier implies five- to six-figure ACV for larger OEM relationships.

## Traction
"1000s of systems deployed" per website (undated claim, sourced from tangramvision.com, 2025). $1.52M raised; no revenue figures public. NASA Phase I SBIR award of $143,264 in August 2024 for lunar terrain vehicle sensor work. No follow-on funding round announced since 2021. Headcount estimated under 20 (LinkedIn, 2025).

## Tech moat
Algorithmic depth in multi-modal calibration pipelines built by Occipital alumni with prior depth-sensor IP. The moat is moderate: calibration math is well-documented in academic literature, and open-source toolchains (Kalibr, OpenCV, ROS camera_calibration) cover significant ground. Primary defensibility is workflow integration, customer onboarding tooling, and the Enterprise white-label relationship stickiness. No patents confirmed publicly. Data network effect is weak — calibration outputs are per-customer, not pooled.

## Distribution model
Direct SaaS with a credit-based self-serve entry point and an Enterprise direct-sales motion for white-label OEM deals. No reseller or marketplace channel identified. Consulting services act as a land-and-expand mechanism into engineering teams.

## Critical weakness
Five years post-founding on $1.52M with no public follow-on round signals either very capital-efficient growth or stalled scale. The open-source calibration ecosystem (Kalibr, ROS tooling) creates a strong "good enough" ceiling that limits willingness to pay for many teams. With a small team and no announced Series A, the company may lack the GTM capacity to move upmarket into large OEM production-line deals where the ROI is clearest.

## Adjacent gap
MetriCal targets English-speaking, ROS-native robotics engineers at funded startups and defense primes. It does not visibly serve Asian manufacturing OEMs (Japan, South Korea, Taiwan) building industrial arms, AMRs, or collaborative robots — a large and fast-growing segment with calibration needs that use proprietary middleware stacks rather than ROS. A wedge here would require localization, integration with non-ROS motion controllers (e.g., FANUC CNC, Mitsubishi MELFA), and a channel partnership with a regional systems integrator — work Tangram Vision has not signaled it is doing.
