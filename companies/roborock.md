# Roborock
**Vertical:** Domestic / consumer robotics | **URL:** https://global.roborock.com | **HQ:** Beijing, China (R&D also in Shanghai, Shenzhen) | **Founded:** 2014 | **Stage/Funding:** Public — listed on Shanghai STAR Market (688169) Feb 2020; market cap ~$4.4B (Apr 2026)

## Product
Roborock sells a tiered range of robot vacuum-mop combos (Q, Qrevo, Saros series), cordless stick vacuums, and wet-dry vacuums, with robotic lawn mowers (RockMow, RockNeo) added in late 2025. Flagship models (Saros Z70, Qrevo Curv 2) feature LiDAR SLAM navigation, ultrasonic carpet detection, self-emptying/self-washing dock stations, and — on the Z70 — a robotic arm for obstacle relocation. Hardware is sold as a one-time purchase; recurring revenue comes from consumables (brushes, filters, mop pads) and optional cloud services.

## ICP
Tech-forward homeowners in mid-to-high income brackets, primarily in China (largest volume), Western Europe, and North America. Average selling price sits in the $400–$1,200 range. Buyers skew toward dual-income households in urban apartments or suburban homes who prioritise app-controlled automation and smart-home integration (Alexa, Google Assistant, Apple Home).

## Traction
2025 revenue: ¥18.7B (~$2.6B USD), up 56.5% YoY — first time exceeding ¥10B. Global robot vacuum market share: 17.7% by shipments, #1 globally for the first time. 20M+ households served (cumulative, per Yicai, Apr 2025). R&D spend: ¥1.42B (~$207M), ~7.6% of revenue.

## Tech moat
Proprietary SLAM stack and sensor fusion (LiDAR + structured light + ToF) built over a decade of floor-plan data gives meaningful navigation accuracy. Manufacturing scale in China yields cost control unavailable to Western OEMs. Strong IP portfolio around dock station self-cleaning and obstacle-avoidance. Roborock's app ecosystem creates mild switching costs via saved maps and automation routines. No meaningful network-effect moat; data flywheel is real but not publicly quantified.

## Distribution model
Primarily direct-to-consumer via own website and app, plus marketplace presence (Amazon, JD.com, Tmall). Retail shelf placement in consumer-electronics chains (Best Buy, MediaMarkt). No RaaS or subscription hardware model. Sales motion is product-led: review-site SEO, influencer/YouTuber unboxing, and Amazon ranking drive the majority of Western conversions.

## Critical weakness
Roborock's entire business is single-category (floor cleaning) and single-channel (B2C hardware). Revenue concentration in China and reliance on Amazon/marketplace algorithms for Western growth make it exposed to tariff shocks, platform-fee changes, and Chinese competitor (Dreame, Narwal, Ecovacs) price wars. Dreame competes at 20–30% lower ASPs with comparable specs, compressing Roborock's premium positioning. No meaningful commercial/B2B revenue cushion.

## Adjacent gap
Roborock does not serve SMB or light-commercial environments (short-term rentals, co-working spaces, small clinics) where daily automated cleaning has clear ROI but consumer robots lack the durability, fleet management, and compliance documentation operators need. A startup selling a purpose-built fleet of durable robot vacuums with a thin SaaS layer — fleet scheduling, cleaning logs for health-inspection compliance, remote monitoring — could own this wedge before any of the Chinese consumer brands choose to invest in B2B sales infrastructure and longer warranty/service commitments.
