# Doosan Robotics
**Vertical:** Industrial arms / cobots | **URL:** https://www.doosanrobotics.com | **HQ:** Suwon, Gyeonggi Province, South Korea | **Founded:** 2015 | **Stage/Funding:** Public — KOSPI (KRX: 454910) since October 2023; IPO raised ~$317M (421.2B KRW); market cap ~$3B (2024)

## Product
Doosan sells a range of collaborative robots (cobots) across five series — M, A, H, E, and P — covering payloads from 5 kg to 30 kg. The M and A series target general manufacturing and assembly; H series handles heavy-duty industrial tasks; E series (launched April 2023) is purpose-built for food and beverage environments; P series (launched May 2024) targets palletizing at 30 kg payload. All hardware ships with the DART-Suite software ecosystem, which includes a no-code drag-and-drop programming environment (Dart-Lab), offline simulation (Dart-Studio), an app marketplace (Dr.Dart-Store), and OTA firmware updates. The company also sells specialized service robots (camera robots, modular café robots) but cobots remain the core revenue driver.

## ICP
Mid-to-large manufacturers in automotive, electronics, food and beverage, and logistics. Named customers include Continental, Mahle, Hyundai, Toyota, Samsung C&T, LG Electronics, Lancôme, Dior, and Dyson. Geographic revenue split is roughly 70% North America and Europe, 30% rest of world. Deal sizes are capital-equipment contracts (unit prices typically $30K–$80K per arm plus integration). Doosan holds approximately 72% global market share for cobots with payloads ≥ 20 kg, indicating a tilt toward heavier-duty industrial buyers rather than light-assembly SMEs.

## Traction
~1,400 units sold in 2022 (Wikipedia / company filings). Full-year 2024 revenue: 46.83B KRW (~$34M USD), down 11.7% YoY from 53.04B KRW in 2023. Operating losses widened to 36.56B KRW in 2024. Trailing 12-month revenue as of September 2025: ~$22.3M USD (CBInsights). Deployed in 50+ countries. Ranked 5th globally in cobot market share as of 2022 (Wikipedia).

## Tech moat
Dominant position in high-payload cobots (≥ 20 kg) provides a narrow but real hardware differentiation. DART-Suite's app marketplace creates mild ecosystem lock-in for integrators who build on the SDK. Parent Doosan Group provides manufacturing scale and balance-sheet support. CES 2026 "Best of Innovation" in AI signals investment in software layer. Moat is thin at the platform level — no proprietary data flywheel, no exclusive manufacturing IP publicly disclosed; incumbency and brand in Korea/automotive are the primary advantages.

## Distribution model
Primarily channel-based: global dealer and systems-integrator network ("Find Nearest Dealer"). Direct enterprise accounts for named OEM customers. Regional subsidiaries in Plano TX (2022) and Düsseldorf (2024) support North America and Europe. Acquired ONExia Inc. (Pennsylvania, 2025) to deepen U.S. direct-sales and applications capability. No RaaS offering publicly available; transactions are capital-equipment sales.

## Critical weakness
Revenue is declining while losses are widening — the company is burning cash at scale without a clear path to profitability, which constrains R&D and channel investment. The business is heavily dependent on a handful of large industrial customers in automotive and electronics; a procurement freeze in either segment hits hard. The DART-Suite software ecosystem is undifferentiated versus Universal Robots' PolyScope and is not yet a meaningful switching-cost moat.

## Adjacent gap
Doosan's sales motion, minimum viable payload, and pricing are calibrated for large industrials with dedicated robotics engineers. The underserved wedge is **light-manufacturing SMEs (under 200 employees) in Western Europe and North America** — food processing, craft manufacturing, small-batch electronics assembly — that need cobots with payloads under 10 kg but lack integration teams and cannot justify $40K+ capital spend plus SI fees. A credible attacker could lead with a RaaS / subscription model, pre-built task-specific configurations (pick-and-place, quality inspection, packaging), and a zero-code setup experience — addressing the deployment cost and skills gap that Doosan's channel ignores.
