# 1X Technologies
**Vertical:** General-purpose humanoids | **URL:** https://www.1x.tech | **HQ:** Palo Alto, CA (manufacturing in Hayward, CA and Moss, Norway) | **Founded:** 2014 (as Halodi Robotics; rebranded 2022) | **Stage/Funding:** Series B — $136.5M total (OpenAI Startup Fund led Series A2 in 2023; EQT Ventures + Samsung NEXT led $100M Series B in January 2024); reportedly seeking $1B at ≥$10B valuation as of September 2025

## Product
Two SKUs: **EVE**, a wheeled humanoid (25 DoF, 4-hr battery, proprietary tendon-driven Revo1 actuators) deployed in industrial security, logistics, and healthcare; and **NEO Gamma**, a 66-lb bipedal humanoid targeting residential use, priced at $20,000 purchase or $499/month subscription, with pre-orders open since October 2025 and delivery scheduled for 2026. NEO currently relies heavily on human teleoperation via VR headset to collect training data; autonomous capability is being developed via an onboard 160M-parameter vision-language transformer ("Redwood AI") and a world model trained on thousands of hours of manipulation video from EVE deployments.

## ICP
EVE: enterprise buyers in security, logistics, and healthcare — primarily North America and Northern Europe — on RaaS contracts (ADT Security at ~$50K/unit/year is the anchor customer; also Altopack, Strongpoint, Sunnaas Hospital, I-Mens). NEO: early-adopter consumers and tech-forward households globally, initially limited to waitlist pilots. Deal size for industrial: six-figure annual contracts; consumer: $20K one-time or ~$6K/year subscription.

## Traction
~$7M annual revenue from EVE leasing to ADT (2022 figure; 140-unit agreement). Pilot deployments reportedly up ~200% between 2024–2025 across Northern Europe and North America. NEO pre-orders open; no unit sales figures disclosed. 663 employees as of September 2025. 40M+ YouTube views on a November 2024 cooking demonstration. Source: Contrary Research, 2025; Wikipedia; BusinessWire, December 2025.

## Tech moat
Proprietary tendon-driven, cable-driven actuation (no harmonic gears) enabling safer human-robot proximity. Embodied AI flywheel: EVE industrial deployments generate real-world manipulation data that trains the world model for NEO. Vertical integration (in-house manufacturing Norway + California) allows hardware iteration. Thin on software moat — world model and Redwood AI are early-stage; leadership has acknowledged current AI "completely fails at most reasoning tasks."

## Distribution model
Direct enterprise sales for EVE (RaaS contracts). Consumer NEO via direct DTC — public waitlist, pre-order page, subscription or outright purchase. Strategic partnership with EQT to supply up to 10,000 NEO units to EQT's 300+ portfolio companies (manufacturing, warehousing, logistics) between 2026–2030. Sales motion is relationship-driven at enterprise level; consumer relies on media and viral demonstrations.

## Critical weakness
NEO is not yet meaningfully autonomous — teleoperation by human operators is the current reality, which means unit economics and scalability are unproven. The company is simultaneously trying to nail consumer hardware, consumer AI, and industrial RaaS, spreading execution across three hard problems. Safety and privacy incidents in residential settings could catastrophically slow consumer adoption before the AI flywheel has time to spin up.

## Adjacent gap
The EQT partnership targets large enterprise portfolio companies (300+ firms, manufacturing/warehousing/logistics scale). What's unaddressed: **SME manufacturers and 3PL operators in emerging markets** — particularly Southeast Asia and Eastern Europe — that need flexible manipulation labor but cannot afford Tesla Optimus or Figure AI pricing, lack the IT infrastructure for enterprise RaaS contracts, and are ignored by US/Norway-centric sales motions. A startup that right-sized a humanoid or dexterous-arm solution for sub-$10K/year lease economics and deployed with a local integrator channel in Vietnam, Poland, or Mexico could build defensible regional density before the majors localize. The wedge is price point + channel, not hardware novelty.
