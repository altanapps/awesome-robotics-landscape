# Spacer Robotics
**Vertical:** Construction robotics (rebar automation) | **URL:** https://www.spacerrobotics.ai/ | **HQ:** San Francisco, CA | **Founded:** ~2024 (emerged from stealth Sep 2025) | **Stage/Funding:** Pre-seed / seed — no public raise disclosed

## Product
GRID is an autonomous mobile robot that ties rebar on construction sites. It navigates rebar grids using SLAM and LiDAR, auto-feeds wire reels, tracks tie counts via RGB camera and smart sensors, and runs for 14 hours on a standard outlet charge. The product targets one of the most physically demanding, repetitive tasks in concrete formwork. The company's longer-term roadmap layers in a humanoid platform (Andromeda Gen 2) framed around lunar construction, but GRID is the only commercialized SKU as of the Sep 2025 launch.

## ICP
General contractors and concrete subcontractors on mid-to-large commercial or infrastructure projects in the US that use significant reinforced concrete (high-rises, bridges, data centers, stadiums). Geography appears US-first given SF HQ and no international signals. Deal size not public; comparable RaaS hardware in construction runs $3K–$10K/month per unit.

## Traction
No public revenue, customer names, or deployment count disclosed as of Sep 2025 launch event. The press release marks the product debut — no pilots or contracts announced. n/a.

## Tech moat
Sensor-fusion navigation tuned for the irregular, cluttered geometry of rebar grids is non-trivial; SLAM in that environment is harder than flat-floor warehouses. Lesya Hendrix (CEO) brings relevant hardware pedigree from Tesla, Scania, and Nuro. No patents, proprietary data assets, or exclusive supply agreements are public. Moat is thin at this stage — primarily founder domain knowledge and any operational data accumulated from early deployments.

## Distribution model
Appears direct sales targeting GCs and concrete subs. No channel or OEM partners announced. Business model not formally disclosed; RaaS is the likely motion given hardware capex and the need for on-site support.

## Critical weakness
The company launched from stealth with a product demo but no public customer traction, revenue, or channel. Construction buyers are famously slow-moving and risk-averse — a single-task robot with no reference sites will face a long, expensive pilot cycle. Capital requirements for hardware iteration and field deployment are high relative to what a pre-seed company can absorb. The lunar narrative, while attention-grabbing, may confuse enterprise buyers and blur the pitch.

## Adjacent gap
GRID targets US commercial construction for rebar tying — one task, one geography, one material type. The wedge a competitor could attack: **rebar automation for civil-infrastructure and bridge contractors in markets with acute labor shortages outside the US** (UK, Australia, Canada, Gulf states), where unionization friction is lower and government-funded infrastructure pipelines are large and predictable. Alternatively, a multi-task formwork robot (tie + inspect + survey in one platform) sold to mid-tier regional concrete subs who cannot justify a single-task SKU would outflank GRID on ROI per square foot.
