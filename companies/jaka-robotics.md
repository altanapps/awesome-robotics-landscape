# JAKA Robotics
**Vertical:** Industrial arms / cobots | **URL:** https://www.jaka.com | **HQ:** Shanghai, China (Minhang District) | **Founded:** 2014 | **Stage/Funding:** Series D — $224M total raised (latest round $150M, July 2022; investors include SoftBank Vision Fund 2, Temasek, Aramco's Prosperity7)

## Product
JAKA sells 6-axis collaborative robot arms across five hardware families: Zu (3–35 kg payload, general-purpose), Pro (high-precision industrial), S (force-sensing variants), A (all-in-one with integrated controller), and Mini/MiniCobo (lightweight, service/education). A newer embodied robotics line (Kargo, K1, Lumi) targets mobile + manipulation use cases. Arms ship with integrated vision systems, force/torque sensors, and a proprietary controller. Pricing sits sub-$30K for entry models, positioning against Universal Robots on price.

## ICP
Mid-to-large manufacturers in automotive, electronics, new energy (EV/battery), and home appliances. Primary geography is China; secondary push into Southeast Asia (Malaysia), Japan, Germany, and the US (Phoenix office). Typical deal is a multi-arm line deployment through a systems integrator, not a single-unit sale.

## Traction
10,000+ cobots deployed globally as of mid-2022 (company statement at Series D). Robot sales more than doubled in H1 2022 vs. prior year. 100+ channel partners in the JAKA+ ecosystem. No public revenue figure post-2018 ($29M reported then); no updated figure confirmed as of April 2026. (Sources: TechFundingNews 2022, Tracxn, Crunchbase)

## Tech moat
Nine proprietary core algorithms (collision detection, force control, path planning) differentiate from white-label Chinese OEM arms. Manufacturing scale in Shanghai gives cost advantage vs. Western cobots. SoftBank/Temasek backing provides distribution reach in Japan and Southeast Asia. No evidence of a unique dataset moat or regulatory lock-in outside China.

## Distribution model
Hybrid: direct enterprise sales to named accounts (automotive OEMs, electronics fabs) plus a channel partner network (JAKA+) of 100+ system integrators and resellers. Regional offices handle local relationships in DE, JP, MY, US. No RaaS or marketplace model observed.

## Critical weakness
JAKA's growth engine is China's domestic manufacturing boom — over 50% of global cobot sales now come from Chinese brands, creating severe domestic price compression. Outside China, JAKA lacks the brand trust, local support density, and safety certification footprint of Universal Robots or ABB. Their Series D was July 2022; no public follow-on in nearly four years, which may signal slower-than-projected international traction or a delayed IPO path.

## Adjacent gap
JAKA does not credibly serve Western SMEs (sub-50-person manufacturers, job shops, contract electronics assemblers) that need a cobot with English-first UX, local in-country support SLAs, and pre-built integrations with SME software stacks (ERP/MES tools like Fishbowl, JobBOSS, Katana). A startup could attack the UK/EU SME job-shop segment — think 5–30 employees, high-mix/low-volume production — with a cobot-as-a-service model wrapping a white-label arm in deployment services, remote monitoring, and per-part pricing. JAKA (and UR) both optimise for volume deployments at large factories; the sub-10-arm, high-churn SME buyer is structurally underserved.
