# GreyOrange
**Vertical:** Warehouse / fulfillment automation | **URL:** https://www.greyorange.com | **HQ:** Suwanee, GA (global HQ relocated from India/Singapore ~2021) | **Founded:** 2012 | **Stage/Funding:** Series D — $545M total raised; $135M Series D closed Dec 2023 (led by Anthelion Capital)

## Product
GreyOrange sells an AI-driven warehouse orchestration platform (GreyMatter) bundled with a proprietary fleet of Autonomous Mobile Robots (Ranger series) and an expanding certified third-party robot network. GreyMatter coordinates goods-to-person robots, autonomous forklifts, intralogistics AMRs, and human workers in real time using multi-agent AI. They also sell gStore (RFID-based in-store inventory) and gNetwork (supply-chain intelligence). Delivery is primarily Robotics-as-a-Service (RaaS), bundling hardware, software, and 24/7 support into recurring OpEx contracts.

## ICP
Large-enterprise retailers, 3PLs, fashion/apparel brands, and grocery chains in North America, Europe, and Asia-Pacific. Named customers include Walmart, H&M, GXO Logistics, Fabletics, DISH Network, Dorman, and Apotek Hjärtat. Deal sizes imply multi-million-dollar, multi-year contracts typical of Fortune 500 procurement cycles. Geography skews toward US and EU with growing presence in India and APAC.

## Traction
100,000+ physical agents deployed across 3,000+ active global sites (company-reported, 2025). Revenue: ₹340.4 crore (~$41M USD) for FY2023–24 with ₹10.8 crore profit after tax (India entity filing). Named the leading global AMR vendor by a third-party market analysis in 2023. ~1,051 employees as of early 2026.

## Tech moat
GreyMatter's multi-agent orchestration software is the primary differentiator — it can manage heterogeneous robot fleets (own + third-party via Certified Ranger Network), which creates integration lock-in. Scale of deployment data (1M+ optimizations/minute) provides training signal advantage. SOC 2 Type II and ISO 27001 certifications reduce friction with enterprise security reviews. Hardware manufacturing moat is moderate; robots are modular and some are third-party.

## Distribution model
Direct enterprise sales with long procurement cycles. RaaS subscription model for recurring revenue. Expanding channel via the Certified Ranger Network (ecosystem partners bring compatible robots into GreyMatter). Bailey International partnership (2025) signals channel/integrator motion entering.

## Critical weakness
Revenue (~$41M USD) is small relative to $545M raised, indicating heavy burn and long payback cycles. Dependence on large, slow enterprise deals creates lumpy pipeline and vulnerability to budget freezes. The open-API / Certified Ranger Network strategy risks commoditizing their hardware differentiation while competitors (Locus, Geek+, Symbotic) also chase the orchestration layer.

## Adjacent gap
GreyOrange is structurally locked out of mid-market and SMB warehouses: their RaaS contracts require long procurement cycles, large minimum deployments, and enterprise IT integration that operators with 50,000–200,000 sq ft facilities cannot afford or staff. A credible wedge is a **plug-and-play AMR system for mid-market 3PLs and regional e-commerce fulfillment centers** in the US and UK — standardized hardware, no-code setup, monthly subscription under $15K, with onboarding measured in days not months. This segment is large, underserved by the enterprise incumbents, and increasingly pressured by labor costs post-COVID.
