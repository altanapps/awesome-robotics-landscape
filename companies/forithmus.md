## Product
Forithmus builds generalist AI systems that interpret medical images — CT scans, MRIs, and other 3D imaging modalities — by training on large-scale datasets pairing volumetric scans with radiology reports and clinical data. Unlike narrow point-solution classifiers (e.g. a single-disease detector), the product aims to be a broad "medical intelligence" layer. Offerings include a radiology suite, a model API, and a research hub, suggesting both enterprise deployment and developer/research access.

## ICP
Radiologists and physicians at healthcare institutions globally. Secondary ICP: researchers and institutions in underserved geographies with limited specialist access. Deal size unknown; API access suggests a tiered model (research vs. enterprise).

## Traction
No public revenue or deployment figures. Investors noted founders "achieved significant results before raising a single euro" — implies working prototypes or pilot deployments, but nothing citable. Pre-seed stage, $4M raised (source: The Disruptor Magazine / LinkedIn, ~late 2024).

## Tech moat
Large-scale training datasets combining 3D imaging, radiology reports, and clinical context. Founders hold hybrid MD + CS backgrounds, which is rare. Moat is thin at this stage — foundation model differentiation in medical imaging is contested (Rad-DINO, Med-Gemini, and others are well-resourced incumbents).

## Distribution model
Likely direct enterprise sales to hospitals and radiology networks, plus API/research hub for developer access. Sales motion unknown; pre-commercial stage.

## Critical weakness
Competes directly with well-capitalized foundation model efforts from Google DeepMind (Med-Gemini), Microsoft/Nuance, and academic spinouts. Regulatory clearance (FDA 510(k), CE mark) is a long, expensive path. $4M pre-seed is thin for a compute- and data-intensive foundation model play.
