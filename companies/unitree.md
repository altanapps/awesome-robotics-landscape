# Unitree
**Vertical:** General-purpose humanoids | **URL:** https://www.unitree.com | **HQ:** Hangzhou, China | **Founded:** 2016 | **Stage/Funding:** Series C (unicorn, ~$1.7B valuation mid-2025); filed Shanghai STAR Market IPO March 2026 targeting ¥4.2B (~$610M)

## Product
Unitree sells a stacked hardware portfolio across four categories: humanoid robots (G1 at ~$13,500–$16,000; H1 at ~$90,000; H1-2; H2; R1), quadruped robots (Go2, B2, AlienGo, and variants), dexterous hands (Dex series), and robotic arms (Z1, D1-T). The G1 is the volume driver — a sub-$16K humanoid aimed at researchers, developers, and small labs. The H1/H2 line targets industrial pilots at enterprise price points. All units are sold as hardware; SDK and developer tools are bundled. No public RaaS offering.

## ICP
Primary buyers are research institutions and universities (estimated 70%+ of 2025 humanoid revenue), robotics startups, and government/defense-adjacent labs. Secondary segment is light industrial pilots (inspection, logistics). Geography skewed domestic China in 2025 after being export-majority (55%+) through 2024. Deal sizes range from ~$13K (G1 developer unit) to $90K+ (H1 enterprise). No disclosed enterprise contract values.

## Traction
2025 revenue: ¥1.71B (~$250M), up from ¥392M (~$57M) in 2024 (336% growth). Net profit 2025: ~¥600M (~$90M), up 674% YoY — profitable, unlike most peers. Humanoid unit shipments: ~5,500 in 2025; targeting 20,000 in 2026. Humanoid revenue crossed 50% of total sales in Q1–Q3 2025, overtaking quadrupeds. Cumulative quadruped sales: 30,000+ units. Sources: Rest of World (2026), Humanoids Daily (2026).

## Tech moat
Manufacturing cost advantage is the primary moat — G1 at $16K undercuts Western humanoid competitors by 5–10x. Vertical integration in Hangzhou factory. Proprietary VLA model (UnifoLM-VLA-0, Jan 2026) scored 98.7 on LIBERO benchmark, highest published in category. Large installed base of 30,000+ quadrupeds generates real-world locomotion data. IP portfolio is real but not independently audited. Regulatory moat is thin outside China.

## Distribution model
Direct sales via unitree.com and regional authorized distributors. No disclosed channel or OEM partnerships at scale. Developer/researcher segment is largely self-serve (shop.unitree.com). Enterprise H1 sales appear to involve a direct sales motion (contact-for-price). Domestic China sales now exceed exports following 2025 shift.

## Critical weakness
Nvidia chip dependency for inference compute (~20% of raw material imports flagged as geopolitically exposed in IPO filing). A US export restriction tightening on advanced chips could crimp production or force costly re-tooling. Secondary risk: 100+ Chinese humanoid competitors are consolidating — Unitree's cost lead narrows as peers reach similar manufacturing scale.

## Adjacent gap
Unitree's customer base is overwhelmingly research/developer (academic labs, robotics startups) — they do not have a validated commercial deployment in any single vertical at scale. The wedge: a startup that takes the G1 hardware (or any low-cost Chinese platform) and builds a vertically integrated software + services layer for one specific industrial use case — e.g., UK/EU warehouse last-metre handling or care-home assistance — where Unitree has no local presence, no regulatory approvals, and no sector-specific training data. The gap is not the hardware; it is the last-mile deployment stack, compliance, and customer success infrastructure that converts a $16K developer kit into a recurring enterprise contract in a regulated Western market.
