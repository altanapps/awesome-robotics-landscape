# John Deere
**Vertical:** Agricultural robotics | **URL:** https://www.deere.com | **HQ:** Moline, Illinois, USA | **Founded:** 1837 | **Stage/Funding:** Public (NYSE: DE); ~$130B market cap (May 2025). Blue River Technology acquired for $305M in 2017.

## Product
John Deere's autonomy segment centers on three shipping or near-shipping products. The **Autonomous 8R/9RX Tractor** uses a second-generation 16-camera array for 360° perception and performs driverless tillage; limited kits ship in 2025, full release in 2026. **See & Spray** (Blue River Technology) applies computer vision to detect weeds at the plant level and spot-spray herbicide, cutting non-residual herbicide use by ~50% across more than 5 million acres as of 2025. The **Autonomous 5ML Orchard Tractor** adds Lidar for dense canopy navigation and handles air-blast spraying; limited availability in 2026. A cloud-based Operations Center Mobile platform ties fleet supervision together. All products are positioned as retrofit kits or factory-ready options on existing iron, not standalone robots.

## ICP
Large-scale row-crop and specialty-crop operators in North America (15+ states confirmed for See & Spray) who already own late-model Deere iron (8R/9RX model year 2020.5+, 9R/9RX model year 2022+). Deal size is enterprise-level capital equipment — retrofit kits on six-figure tractors. Secondary ICPs include commercial landscapers and quarry contractors for newer autonomous products.

## Traction
See & Spray deployed on 5M+ acres in 2025, up 20x from 2022 baseline; autonomous tractors have farmed 50,000+ acres in field testing. Global autonomous tractor fleet is under 50 units as of mid-2025. Deere does not break out autonomy-segment revenue separately. (Sources: PRNewswire CES 2025 announcement; Klover.ai analysis 2025.)

## Tech moat
Blue River's plant-level CV dataset (billions of labeled weed images, now 5M+ acres of in-field inference) is the primary moat — replicating it requires years of field seasons. Deere's dealer network (~4,000 North American locations) creates a service and activation lock-in that pure-software competitors cannot easily replicate. Existing Deere hardware installed base means autonomy kits have a built-in addressable market. Patents on perception stack and machine-learning herbicide application add IP protection.

## Distribution model
Dealer-led: autonomy kits sold and activated through ~4,000 authorized Deere dealers. Hardware bundled or retrofitted to existing Deere tractors; software managed via Operations Center cloud subscription. No RaaS model publicly available. Sales motion is consultative/direct through dealer relationship, not self-serve.

## Critical weakness
Right-to-repair lock-in is the single biggest strategic liability. Farmers are legally and practically prevented from self-servicing or using independent shops, concentrating repair margin with dealers and generating intense political and regulatory backlash. If right-to-repair legislation passes at scale, it erodes aftersales margin and weakens the dealer-network moat simultaneously. Additionally, the autonomous fleet is under 50 units globally as of 2025 — the autonomy program is still pre-scale and operationally unproven at commercial volume.

## Adjacent gap
The entire autonomy stack is designed for large North American row-crop farms running 8R/9RX-class tractors — operations of 1,000+ acres with six-figure equipment budgets. **Smallholder and mid-sized farms (50–500 acres), specialty crop producers (vegetables, berries, vineyards), and farmers in Europe, Latin America, and Asia-Pacific are entirely unserved.** These operators cannot afford Deere hardware, cannot use the retrofit kits on non-Deere equipment, and have heterogeneous crop geometries that Deere's current CV models don't cover. A startup targeting sub-500-acre specialty crop operations with hardware-agnostic, crop-specific autonomy (e.g., autonomous scouting or precision spraying on compact tractors or purpose-built platforms) faces no credible Deere competition and addresses a larger global unit count than the large-farm segment.
