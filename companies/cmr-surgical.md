# CMR Surgical
**Vertical:** Surgical / medical robotics | **URL:** https://cmrsurgical.com | **HQ:** Cambridge, UK (manufacturing in Ely, Cambridgeshire) | **Founded:** 2014 | **Stage/Funding:** Private, unicorn ($3B valuation as of 2021); $1.35B total raised; most recent round >$200M (equity + Trinity Capital debt, April 2025)

## Product
CMR Surgical sells Versius, a modular soft-tissue robotic surgery system composed of independent arm carts that position around the patient table rather than a fixed overhead tower. Each arm cart stores compactly and can be reconfigured per procedure. The next-generation Versius Plus received FDA 510(k) clearance in early 2026 for cholecystectomy, with U.S. commercial launch planned for 2026. Cleared indications span colorectal, general, gynecology, thoracic, and urology; pediatric and transoral (TORS) trials are active. The system competes explicitly on price and footprint against Intuitive Surgical's da Vinci.

## ICP
Mid-to-large acute-care hospitals that run high laparoscopic volume across multiple specialties and face OR space or multi-robot scheduling constraints. Primary push is now the U.S. market post-FDA clearance; established base spans 30+ countries with strongest penetration in Europe and select Asia-Pacific markets. Deal size is capital-equipment scale ($1–2.5M per system per public estimates), with competitive pricing pitched as a differentiator.

## Traction
45,000+ procedures completed globally as of early 2025 (company-cited; second most-utilized soft-tissue surgical robot worldwide). Installations in 30+ countries. No public revenue disclosed. Raised >$200M in April 2025 specifically to fund U.S. commercialization. Some centers have logged 1,500–2,000 cases on Versius, indicating real clinical depth at anchor sites. (Sources: GlobeNewswire April 2025; MedTech Dive 2025; The Robot Report 2025.)

## Tech moat
Modular arm architecture (IP around independent arm carts) and compact form factor are the primary differentiators. FDA de novo clearance (October 2024) as the regulatory foundation, with 510(k) pathway for indication expansion, creates meaningful but not insurmountable regulatory moat in the U.S. Procedure-count data accumulating across 30+ countries builds a clinical evidence corpus. Manufacturing is consolidated in Ely, UK. Network effects are limited; moat is thinner than Intuitive's 20-year installed base and ecosystem of trained surgeons and disposables.

## Distribution model
Direct sales and installation via hospital partnerships; company works directly with surgical teams and hospital procurement. No disclosed channel or OEM relationships. Competing on price suggests a capital-sale motion rather than RaaS, though no public confirmation of lease terms. U.S. launch will require building a direct salesforce from scratch against entrenched Intuitive reps.

## Critical weakness
Intuitive Surgical holds ~65–70% global market share with 20+ years of surgeon training entrenchment, a vast disposables revenue stream, and clinical data CMR cannot yet match in volume. Versius procedures have longer OR time than standard laparoscopy, which is a cost and scheduling liability for hospital buyers. CMR also recently underwent a CEO transition (interim leadership), introducing execution risk precisely as it attempts a capital-intensive U.S. launch.

## Adjacent gap
CMR targets the same large-hospital, multi-specialty segment as Intuitive — the contest is incumbent displacement, which is slow and expensive. The underserved wedge is **mid-volume community hospitals and ambulatory surgery centers (ASCs) in the U.S. and mid-income markets (Latin America, Southeast Asia, Middle East)** that cannot justify a $2M+ system but perform thousands of laparoscopic cases annually. A purpose-built, lower-cost robotic system — or a RaaS model priced per-procedure rather than per-capital-purchase — targeting ASCs and district hospitals in these geographies would face neither Intuitive's lock-in nor CMR's overhead. The specific use case to anchor: single-specialty (e.g., cholecystectomy or hernia) robotic assistance at ASC price points.
