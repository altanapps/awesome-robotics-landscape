# maxon group
**Vertical:** Picks-and-shovels | **URL:** https://www.maxongroup.com | **HQ:** Sachseln, Switzerland | **Founded:** 1961 | **Stage/Funding:** Private; CHF 594.7M revenue (2024), previously exceeded CHF 700M (2022/23 peak)

## Product
maxon designs and manufactures high-precision brushless DC motors, planetary and strain-wave gearheads, encoders, and servo controllers, sold both as discrete components and as fully integrated joint actuators. The robotics-specific line (launched ~2024) comprises 15 actuator SKUs split into two families: High Efficiency Joints (EC frameless DT motor + planetary gear + integrated electronics + IP67 housing, up to 86% efficiency) targeting mobile/legged robots, and High Precision Joints (strain-wave gearbox, zero-backlash) targeting cobots and surgical manipulators. All products are manufactured in-house across facilities in Switzerland, Germany, and the US.

## ICP
Primary buyers are OEM robot manufacturers — humanoid and quadruped developers, cobot makers (e.g., collaborative arm OEMs), and surgical robotics companies — who need certified, high-cycle-life actuation rather than commodity motors. Secondary buyers are aerospace/defense primes and medical device companies (implantable pumps, surgical tools). Deals are typically multi-year supply agreements; unit volumes range from hundreds (surgical) to tens of thousands (service robots). Geographic concentration is Europe and North America, with growing Asia-Pacific presence.

## Traction
CHF 594.7M total group revenue for 2024 (down from a peak exceeding CHF 700M in a prior year due to market softening); ~3,200 employees across 41 countries. Medical and robotics cited as key growth segments. No public breakdown by vertical. (Sources: maxon press release 2025, Ceramic Applications 2023.)

## Tech moat
Six decades of precision motor manufacturing know-how with proprietary winding and magnet-assembly processes. Vertical integration from stator winding through electronics and firmware reduces supply-chain risk and enables tight performance tolerances (sub-arc-minute positioning). Space-heritage qualification (Mars rovers, ESA missions) functions as a quality signal that unlocks regulated markets competitors cannot easily enter. Global after-sales and application engineering network across 41 countries reinforces switching costs for OEM customers.

## Distribution model
Primarily direct sales via regional application engineers for large OEM accounts. Supplemented by exclusive regional distributors (e.g., Electromate in Canada) for mid-market and prototyping volume. No self-serve or marketplace channel. Sales motion is consultative and long-cycle — engineering co-development often precedes a supply agreement.

## Critical weakness
Price point and minimum order complexity lock out pre-revenue and early-Series-A robotics startups who need a capable joint but cannot commit to OEM volumes or afford maxon's per-unit cost. Long qualification timelines (weeks to months for application engineering support) also create friction for teams iterating rapidly on hardware. This leaves a structural gap at the low end of the market.

## Adjacent gap
Early-stage humanoid and mobile-manipulation startups — primarily in the US and Asia — need integrated joints that are good enough for prototype-to-pilot scale (10–500 units), priced for pre-revenue burn rates, and available with fast lead times and self-serve documentation. maxon's minimum viable engagement (dedicated application engineer, OEM supply agreement) is architecturally mismatched to this segment. A startup offering a curated, in-stock joint module (brushless motor + strain-wave gear + encoder + FOC driver, single-SKU) with published specs, off-the-shelf pricing, and a developer-community support model could own the "first joint a startup ever buys" position — and convert those customers to larger supply contracts as they scale.
