# Shield AI
**Vertical:** Defense / dual-use robotics | **URL:** https://shield.ai | **HQ:** San Diego, CA | **Founded:** 2015 | **Stage/Funding:** Series G, ~$2B raised, $12.7B valuation (2026)

## Product
Shield AI sells Hivemind Enterprise — a modular AI autonomy software stack (EdgeOS, Hivemind Pilot, Hivemind Commander, Hivemind Forge) that enables aircraft to operate in GPS- and comms-denied environments using reinforcement learning, real-time planning, and multi-agent coordination. It is licensed as a developer platform to OEMs and sovereign customers. The company also sells and operates its own hardware: the V-BAT VTOL UAS (ISR, maritime), the Nova 2 quadrotor, and the X-BAT autonomous VTOL fighter (first flight targeted 2026). Contractor-operated ISR services (COCO contracts) constitute a third revenue line.

## ICP
Primary buyers are national militaries and government agencies via direct defense procurement and foreign military sales (FMS). Key customers include US DoD (USMC, Coast Guard, Navy), Japan MSDF, Royal Netherlands Navy, India Army, Armenia, Singapore, South Korea, and Frontex. Deal sizes range from ~$7M SBIR awards to multi-hundred-million IDIQ contracts; ceiling values on framework contracts reach $46B (EWACC). Secondary ICP: large defense primes (L3Harris, General Atomics, Kratos, Airbus) licensing Hivemind for integration into their own platforms.

## Traction
~$300M revenue for the twelve months ending March 2025 (up from $267M in 2024); projecting >$540M for 2026 (~80% YoY growth). V-BAT has logged 130+ combat sorties in Ukraine (since June 2024). Hivemind is deployed on platforms from eight of the top 25 US defense primes. US Navy awarded an $800M contractor-operated ISR contract in April 2026. Sources: Fortune (March 2026), Contrary Research, The Defense Post (April 2026).

## Tech moat
Hivemind's core moat is operational data from real GPS/comms-denied deployments (Ukraine, Israel) — adversarial conditions competitors cannot easily replicate in simulation. Reinforcement-learning expertise was consolidated via acquisition (Heron Systems 2021, Martin UAV 2021, Sentient Vision 2024). The COCO ISR contract model creates a recurring data flywheel. Distribution moat via prime-contractor integrations (eight primes) makes Hivemind a de facto platform dependency. Manufacturing is outsourced; hardware moat is thin relative to the software stack.

## Distribution model
Dual-track: (1) direct government sales and COCO service contracts sold through a dedicated federal business development team; (2) OEM/platform licensing of Hivemind Enterprise to defense primes and sovereign integrators. No commercial marketplace or channel partner model. Sales motion is long-cycle RFP and FMS, supplemented by strategic equity investors (L3Harris, Hanwha) who also serve as distribution partners.

## Critical weakness
Nearly all revenue is US and allied-government procurement. Any slowdown in defense AI budgets, a shift in political appetite for autonomous weapons, or a failed high-profile autonomous engagement would stall growth. The COCO operating model also concentrates execution risk: Shield AI must staff and operate ISR services itself, which is operationally intensive and hard to scale without headcount. Valuation ($12.7B) implies an exit multiple that requires either IPO or a strategic acquirer — both paths are sensitive to defense-spending cycles.

## Adjacent gap
Hivemind targets large, manned or high-cost UAS platforms for national militaries — minimum deal sizes in the millions. The unserved wedge is **small allied and partner-nation militaries and police/border agencies in the Indo-Pacific and Eastern Europe** that cannot afford FMS processes or $18M+ procurement cycles but face identical GPS-denied threat environments. A startup could attack this with a stripped-down autonomy runtime, purpose-built for sub-$50K commercial VTOL hardware, sold through a simpler direct commercial channel (not FMS), with a SaaS licensing model rather than platform-sale or COCO. Shield AI has explicitly deprioritized sub-threshold customers to protect its prime-contract relationships.
