# Toggle Industries
**Vertical:** Construction robotics | **URL:** https://toggle.is | **HQ:** New York, NY (also Zurich office) | **Founded:** ~2016 | **Stage/Funding:** Series A extended — $15M total ($3M seed 2019, $8M Series A 2021, $3M extension 2023)

## Product
Toggle sells automated rebar prefabrication as a service: customers submit structural drawings, and Toggle's robotic cells shape, tie, and weld rebar cages off-site in a fabrication facility, then deliver finished assemblies ready for placement. The company also offers Toggle QC, a software platform with four modular components — Feature Verification, Rebar Localization (3D scanning + computer vision), Calibration Assistant, and Junction Detection — sold to cut-and-bend OEMs and steel fabricators for inline quality control without trained metrology staff.

## ICP
General contractors and rebar fabricators on large-scale concrete construction projects in the northeastern United States (Philadelphia, New York metro documented). Deal sizes are project-based; no per-unit pricing disclosed publicly. Secondary ICP via Toggle QC: cut-and-bend machine OEMs and independent steel fabrication shops seeking automated inspection.

## Traction
All existing customers have placed repeat orders (Alumni Ventures, 2023). Documented customer: Miller Bros. — two repeat projects at Philadelphia Navy Yard; Toggle saved ~500 hours on manhole slabs on one project. Company-claimed efficiency metrics: 90% time reduction and 75% cost reduction versus manual rebar assembly. Revenue figures not publicly disclosed. No Series B announced as of April 2026.

## Tech moat
Proprietary robotic cell configuration for rebar geometry (irregular shapes, custom cages) combined with structured-light scanning for in-process QC. Manufacturing know-how and facility setup (50,000 sq ft production plant) create moderate operational moat. IP filings not publicly detailed. Strategic investor Tokyu Construction (Japanese GC) adds a distribution relationship but no disclosed exclusivity. Moat is moderate — industrial robots are commodity hardware; differentiation is in software, process tuning, and throughput.

## Distribution model
Direct B2B service model: Toggle receives design files, fabricates off-site, and delivers to job sites. Toggle QC is sold direct to OEMs and fabricators. No channel partners, OEM licensing, or marketplace model disclosed. Sales motion appears relationship-driven via GCs and structural engineers; Tokyu Construction provides a potential channel into Japanese market.

## Critical weakness
Geographic concentration in a single fabrication facility limits scalable reach — Toggle must open new plants to serve projects beyond trucking distance, which is capital-intensive and operationally complex. The off-site prefab model also requires BIM-ready customers with digital structural drawings, excluding a large portion of the market that still works from paper or 2D CAD.

## Adjacent gap
Toggle focuses on northeastern US, large concrete GCs with BIM workflows. The unserved wedge is **mid-market fabricators in the Sun Belt and Midwest** — high-volume residential and infrastructure concrete work where labor shortages are acute, digital adoption is lower, and no robotics vendor has built a turnkey prefab service. A competitor offering a mobile or modular robotic cell (rather than a fixed-facility model) that can be deployed on or near the job site, with lighter BIM requirements, could capture customers Toggle structurally cannot serve.
