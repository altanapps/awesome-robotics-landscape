# Dusty Robotics
**Vertical:** Construction robotics | **URL:** https://www.dustyrobotics.com | **HQ:** Mountain View, CA | **Founded:** 2018 | **Stage/Funding:** Series B — $69.3M total ($16.5M Series A Jun 2021, $45M Series B May 2022, lead: Scale Venture Partners); post-money valuation ~$250M

## Product
Dusty sells the FieldPrinter, an autonomous wheeled robot that reads BIM files and prints construction layout lines directly onto jobsite floors at 1/16" accuracy and 600 DPI, outputting 10,000–15,000 sq ft per day with one operator. It replaces manual chalk-line and total-station layout work. The FieldPrint Platform (Gen 2, GA October 2024) bundles the robot, a custom total station, tablet controller, Autodesk Revit/AutoCAD integration via a cloud portal, and ongoing firmware updates. Delivery is RaaS — subscription for high-utilization customers, daily rental (with service wrap) in select markets (N. California, Seattle).

## ICP
Large general contractors and major specialty subcontractors (mechanical, framing) running high-volume commercial construction in North America — data centers, healthcare, industrial, and large mixed-use. Deal size is project-scope-based; subscription tier suits contractors with >75% utilization. Named customers include Turner Construction, Skanska, DPR, Swinerton, Southland Industries, and Performance Contracting Inc.

## Traction
25M sq ft laid out in Q1 2022 alone (TechCrunch, May 2022). GetLatka estimates ~$29.9M ARR with ~100-person team (year not confirmed; treat as directional). No public figure for total customer count.

## Tech moat
Moderate. Core defensibility is the accumulated BIM-to-floor workflow — integrations, portal, and field training reduce switching cost for existing GC relationships. The hardware (robot + total station pairing) creates a modest procurement barrier. No known proprietary IP that competitors cannot replicate with capital; Rugged Robotics and Civ Robotics are active in the same lane. Data flywheel (layout accuracy feedback across sites) is a potential long-run moat but not yet demonstrated publicly.

## Distribution model
Direct RaaS, sold to GC procurement or VDC leads. Sales-assisted motion — account executives plus field success staff embedded on projects. No channel or OEM layer disclosed. Geographic footprint currently North America only.

## Critical weakness
North America exclusivity and GC-only focus leave large markets untouched. The RaaS model requires dense geographic coverage to service hardware, making international expansion capital-intensive. If a well-capitalized competitor (or Hilti/Trimble with existing site-services infrastructure) packages equivalent layout automation into their existing distribution network, Dusty's independent sales motion becomes a structural disadvantage.

## Adjacent gap
Dusty serves large North American GCs on new commercial builds — it does not serve small-to-mid-size contractors, renovation/retrofit projects (where existing structures limit robot navigation), or any market outside North America. The clearest wedge: **SMB general contractors in the EU/UK**, where labour costs for manual layout are equally high, BIM adoption is accelerating via mandate (UK BIM Level 2, EU Public Procurement Directive), and no well-funded local player exists. A lighter, cheaper robot — or a layout-as-a-service bureau model using human operators with robot assist — targeting sub-$50M project GCs in Germany, the Netherlands, or the UK could land without competing head-on with Dusty's enterprise sales motion.
